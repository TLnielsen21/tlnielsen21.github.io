My professional background :

  1. Entrepreneurial project management in real estate, and medical device sales – 17 years

  2. K-16 education – I hold a K-12 Principal license, & K-12 Special Ed Director license, specialties in Literacy & STEM for Gifted Learners, English Lang. Acquisition – ELL students, & School Psychology – Gifted, Autism, .. + similar

During my first few days as a “Davinci Coder,”… I’ve focused on researching Electronic Health Records-EHRs – One of my reasons for learning to code.

  1. Though I recently sold my “Co-founding Board Member” financial interest in a Medical Sales company, I hope to continue my ties to the Med field.

  2. Several physicians & hospital organizations feel overwhelmed about the plethora of components involved with initiating and maintaining effective EHRs.

The email I sent to Boston Children’s Hospital today :

Dr. Kohane, I see that you’ve considered EHR software extensively for electronic health records. After reading some ins and outs of Epic and Cerner EHR software — I came across info about smaller Oroville Hospital, 3 hours north of San Francisco. Their CEO was hopeful that he’d be able to use “Open Source” options — and upon connecting by phone with his exec asst — she said Robert Wentz is not disappointed in this tactic, to date. Seemed to me, that they were ~ 1.5 years into implementation. http://www.forbes.com/sites/zinamoukheiber/2012/08/24/open-source-electronic-health-records-a-cost-solution-for-hospitals/

I read about you, Dr. Kohane, in a Forbes article on EHR topics — and wondered about your thoughts. I’m an educational psychologist / + have K-12 School Principal & Special Educ Director licenses. I’m taking my first computer-programming course — and hope to explore this topic within an “end of course” project. Please share your thoughts.

I’m reading HIMSS.org standards – mainly looking for info about the stages that they articulate regarding Electronic Health Records – EHRs implementation. I noticed from Forbes articles that some software owners took the position that their companies would earn a bundle —versus supporting doctors & hospitals in full-scale transition to EHRs for lower costs, greater accessibilities, and “Free Source” resources.

I hope companies from this industry are transitioning their tactics. I appreciate your thoughts.

Thank you,       Terri Lee Nielsen,        Golden, Colorado
